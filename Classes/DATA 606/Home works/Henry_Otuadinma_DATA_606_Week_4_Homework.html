  1. Suppose you read online that children first count to 10 successfully when they are 32 months old, on average. Answer:

n = 36
x <- 32
mean = 30.69
sd = 4.31
se <- sd / sqrt(n)
z = (mean - x) / se
sigLevel <- 0.10
pnorm(z, mean = 0, sd = 1) * 2
## [1] 0.0682026

Null hypothesis (H0): The average development for a child is μ = 32 months.

Alternate hypothesis (HA): The average development for a child is μ ≠ 32 months.

Since the the p-value (0.0682026) is not equal to the significance level, we reject the Null hypothesis (H0).

  1. Interpret the p-value in context of the hypothesis test and the data.

  1. Calculate a 90% confidence interval for the average age at which gifted children first count to 10 successfully.
lowerbound <- round(mean - 1.64 * se,2)

  1. Do your results from the hypothesis test and the confidence interval agree? Explain.

Answer:
